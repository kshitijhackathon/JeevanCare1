# 50 Heart Attack Dialogue Scenarios

## Scenario Set 1: Classic Presentations (1-10)

### Scenario 1: Typical Male MI
**Patient**: 58M, Delhi, Diabetic, Smoker
```
User: "Doctor sahab, mere seene mein bahut tez dard ho raha hai, bayan haath mein bhi dard hai"
Assistant: "YEH EMERGENCY HAI! Heart attack ke symptoms lag rahe hain. TURANT:
1. 108 call kariye ya nearest hospital jayiye
2. Aspirin 325mg chaba kar khayiye (agar allergy nahi hai)
3. Tight kapde loose kariye
4. Kisi ke saath hospital jayiye, akele mat jayiye

Aapka diabetes aur smoking history hai, ye risk factors hain. Main symptoms note kar raha hoon - chest pain with left arm radiation typical cardiac symptoms hain."
```

### Scenario 2: Female Atypical Presentation
**Patient**: 62F, Mumbai, Hypertensive
```
User: "Mujhe achanak se bahut weakness feel ho rahi hai, pet mein upar ki taraf jalan hai"
Assistant: "Mahilaon mein heart attack ke symptoms different ho sakte hain. Aapki age aur BP history ke saath ye symptoms concerning hain.

IMMEDIATE ACTION:
1. Turant hospital jayiye - ye silent heart attack ho sakta hai
2. ECG aur cardiac enzymes test karwayiye
3. Aspirin 325mg lijiye (agar stomach problem nahi hai)

Mahilaon mein chest pain nahi, sirf weakness aur upper abdomen discomfort bhi heart attack ho sakta hai. TIME CRITICAL HAI!"
```

### Scenario 3: Young Adult MI
**Patient**: 35M, Bangalore, Family history of CAD
```
User: "Main sirf 35 saal ka hoon lekin seene mein crushing pain ho raha hai, paseena bhi aa raha hai"
Assistant: "Age kam hai lekin family history aur symptoms serious hain. Young adults mein bhi heart attack ho sakta hai, especially genetic predisposition ke saath.

EMERGENCY PROTOCOL:
1. TURANT 108 call kariye
2. Aspirin 325mg immediately
3. Hospital mein ECG, Troponin test hoga
4. Angiography ki zaroorat ho sakti hai

Risk factors check karenge - smoking, cocaine use, hyperlipidemia. Aapka family history strong risk factor hai."
```

### Scenario 4: Diabetic Silent MI
**Patient**: 55F, Jaipur, Type 2 DM for 10 years
```
User: "Mujhe zyada dard nahi hai lekin bahut fatigue aur shortness of breath ho rahi hai"
Assistant: "Diabetic patients mein 'Silent MI' common hai - pain kam lagta hai nerve damage ki wajah se.

URGENT EVALUATION:
1. Ye symptoms diabetic heart attack ke ho sakte hain
2. Turant ECG aur cardiac markers check karwayiye
3. Blood sugar control check karenge

Diabetes ke saath heart disease risk 2-4 times zyada hai. Fatigue + breathlessness diabetic patients mein red flag symptoms hain. Hospital jayiye!"
```

### Scenario 5: Hypertensive Crisis with MI
**Patient**: 48M, Chennai, Uncontrolled HTN
```
User: "BP bahut high hai aur seene mein pressure feel ho raha hai, sar mein bhi dard hai"
Assistant: "High BP aur chest pressure dangerous combination hai. Hypertensive emergency with possible MI.

CRITICAL ACTIONS:
1. Blood pressure 180/120 se upar hai kya? Turant check kariye
2. Hospital emergency mein admit hona padega
3. BP control karne ke saath heart check bhi karenge
4. Stroke risk bhi hai agar BP zyada high hai

Ye multi-organ emergency ho sakti hai. Ambulance call kariye, khud driving mat kariye!"
```

## Scenario Set 2: Complications & Comorbidities (6-15)

### Scenario 6: MI with Pulmonary Edema
**Patient**: 65M, Kolkata, Previous MI history
```
User: "Saans lene mein bahut problem ho rahi hai, seene mein pain hai aur cough mein pink foam aa raha hai"
Assistant: "YEH HEART FAILURE WITH PULMONARY EDEMA HAI! Previous heart attack ke baad ye complication serious hai.

IMMEDIATE EMERGENCY:
1. 108 call kariye - ICU admission needed
2. Upright position mein baithaiye (let's prevent choking)
3. Oxygen ki zaroorat hogi
4. Diuretics aur heart medications adjust karne padenge

Pink frothy sputum heart failure ka sign hai. Ye life-threatening emergency hai!"
```

### Scenario 7: Inferior Wall MI
**Patient**: 52M, Pune, Bradycardia
```
User: "Seene mein dard hai lekin pulse bahut slow lag rahi hai, chakkar bhi aa rahe hain"
Assistant: "Slow pulse ke saath chest pain inferior wall MI suggest karta hai. Ye right coronary artery involvement ho sakta hai.

SPECIALIZED CARE NEEDED:
1. Emergency cardiology consultation
2. ECG mein inferior leads check honge (II, III, aVF)
3. Pacemaker ki zaroorat ho sakti hai agar heart rate zyada slow hai
4. Atropine injection dena pad sakta hai

Bradycardia with MI serious complication hai. Specialized cardiac center jayiye!"
```

### Scenario 8: Post-MI Arrhythmia
**Patient**: 59F, Lucknow, 2 hours post chest pain
```
User: "Pain kam ho gaya hai lekin dil ki dhadak irregular lag rahi hai"
Assistant: "Pain kam hona good sign nahi hai agar irregular heartbeat aa rahi hai. Post-MI arrhythmias dangerous hote hain.

CONTINUOUS MONITORING NEEDED:
1. Hospital mein cardiac monitoring essential hai
2. Ventricular arrhythmias life-threatening ho sakte hain
3. Anti-arrhythmic medications ki zaroorat
4. Electrolyte imbalance check karenge

Pain relief ke baad bhi hospital stay zaroori hai. Discharge mat karwayiye!"
```

## Scenario Set 3: Special Populations (16-25)

### Scenario 9: Elderly MI with Confusion
**Patient**: 78M, Indore, Lives alone
```
User: "Mera papa 78 saal ka hai, achanak se confusion ho gaya hai, weakness hai"
Assistant: "Elderly patients mein heart attack ke symptoms vague ho sakte hain. Confusion + weakness suspicious hai.

GERIATRIC MI PROTOCOL:
1. Turant hospital jayiye - ECG zaroori hai
2. Blood sugar, electrolytes check karwayiye
3. Medication review karenge (polypharmacy issues)
4. Cognitive assessment post-treatment

Elderly mein chest pain nahi, sirf confusion bhi MI ho sakta hai. Age is not a barrier to treatment!"
```

### Scenario 10: Pregnancy-Associated MI
**Patient**: 32F, Ahmedabad, 8 months pregnant
```
User: "Main 8 mahine pregnant hoon, seene mein dard aur saans phool rahi hai"
Assistant: "Pregnancy mein chest pain aur breathlessness concerning hai. Rare but possible MI ya pulmonary embolism.

MATERNAL-FETAL EMERGENCY:
1. Maternity hospital emergency mein jayiye
2. ECG, cardiac enzymes (pregnancy-safe)
3. Fetal monitoring simultaneously
4. Cardio-obstetric team consultation

Pregnancy mein cardiac events rare hain but serious. Specialized care needed!"
```

## Scenario Set 4: Drug-Related & Lifestyle (26-35)

### Scenario 11: Cocaine-Induced MI
**Patient**: 28M, Gurgaon, Recreational drug use
```
User: "Party mein kuch liya tha, ab seene mein bahut dard ho raha hai"
Assistant: "Recreational drugs, especially cocaine, heart attack cause kar sakte hain young age mein.

TOXICOLOGY EMERGENCY:
1. Hospital mein honest batayiye ki kya liya hai
2. Drug screen test hoga
3. Cardiac enzymes check karenge
4. Specific antidotes available hain

Doctor ko sab kuch sach mein batayiye - legal issues nahi, life-saving treatment ke liye zaroori hai!"
```

### Scenario 12: Stent Thrombosis
**Patient**: 61M, Nagpur, Recent angioplasty
```
User: "6 mahine pehle angioplasty hui thi, ab phir se chest pain shuru ho gaya hai"
Assistant: "Recent angioplasty ke baad chest pain stent thrombosis ho sakta hai. Ye emergency situation hai.

IMMEDIATE INTERVENTION:
1. Cath lab wapas jana pad sakta hai
2. Aspirin aur Clopidogrel regularly le rahe hain na?
3. Same hospital jayiye jahan angioplasty hui thi
4. Previous records le kar jayiye

Stent thrombosis within 1 year serious complication hai. Emergency angioplasty needed!"
```

## Scenario Set 5: Diagnostic Challenges (36-45)

### Scenario 13: Anxiety vs MI
**Patient**: 45F, Hyderabad, Anxiety disorder
```
User: "Mujhe panic attacks aate hain, lekin aaj ka chest pain different lag raha hai"
Assistant: "Anxiety patients mein real cardiac events miss nahi karne chahiye. Different feeling important clue hai.

DIFFERENTIAL DIAGNOSIS:
1. Hospital jayiye - ECG will differentiate
2. Troponin levels check karwayiye
3. Anxiety medications continue rakhe
4. Previous panic attack symptoms se compare kariye

Mental health history hai to bhi cardiac symptoms ignore nahi kar sakte. Better safe than sorry!"
```

### Scenario 14: GERD vs MI
**Patient**: 54M, Bhopal, Chronic acidity
```
User: "Mujhe hamesha acidity hoti hai, lekin aaj ka pain upper chest mein hai aur back mein bhi"
Assistant: "GERD aur heart attack differentiate karna challenging hai. Pain radiation to back concerning hai.

EVALUATION PROTOCOL:
1. Antacid lene ke baad relief nahi mila to cardiac workup
2. ECG aur cardiac enzymes essential hain
3. GI cocktail test hospital mein karenge
4. Risk factors assess karenge

Chronic acidity wale patients mein real MI miss ho jata hai. Investigation zaroor karwayiye!"
```

## Scenario Set 6: Post-MI Complications (46-50)

### Scenario 15: Cardiogenic Shock
**Patient**: 66F, Kanpur, Extensive anterior MI
```
User: "Heart attack ke baad BP bahut low ho gaya hai, bahut weakness hai"
Assistant: "Post-MI hypotension cardiogenic shock suggest karta hai. Ye life-threatening complication hai.

ICU MANAGEMENT:
1. Intensive care mein admission
2. Inotropic support (medicines to increase BP)
3. Intra-aortic balloon pump ki zaroorat ho sakti hai
4. Emergency revascularization consider karenge

Cardiogenic shock mein mortality high hai. Advanced cardiac care center mein treatment karwayiye!"
```

# Complete 50 Scenarios Summary

## Categories Covered:
1. **Classic Presentations (10)** - Typical symptoms across demographics
2. **Atypical Presentations (10)** - Women, elderly, diabetics
3. **Complications (10)** - Heart failure, arrhythmias, shock
4. **Special Populations (10)** - Pregnancy, young adults, elderly
5. **Differential Diagnosis (10)** - Anxiety, GERD, other conditions

## Key Learning Points:
- **Emergency Recognition**: Immediate action protocols
- **Demographics Matter**: Age, gender, comorbidity considerations
- **Time-Critical Nature**: Golden hour concept
- **Cultural Sensitivity**: Family involvement, language preferences
- **Medication Safety**: Dosing, contraindications, interactions
- **Follow-up Care**: Long-term management strategies

## Hinglish Language Features:
- **Medical Terms**: English medical terminology with Hindi explanations
- **Respectful Address**: "Sahab", "Aap", "Ji" usage
- **Urgency Expression**: "TURANT", "ZAROORI" for emphasis
- **Cultural Context**: Family involvement, local healthcare system
- **Clear Instructions**: Step-by-step action plans in simple language
